## 1. Purpose

This rubric evaluates whether an operator-readable explanation of an AquaBlend optimisation result is accurate, safe, complete, understandable, and consistent with the official Results JSON.

The Results JSON is the **single source of truth**. An explanation must not receive a high rating only because it sounds professional. A fluent explanation that contradicts, omits, or invents important information must receive a low score or fail.

This rubric can be used for:

- the reference explanation stored in the Results JSON;
- the deterministic fallback explanation generated by Task 9; and
- future explanations produced by an LLM or another explanation system.

## 2. Evidence Used During Evaluation

The evaluator must compare the explanation against these exact JSON paths where available:

| Explanation area | Primary JSON evidence |
|---|---|
| Scenario and solve status | `scenario_id`, `status` |
| Demand supplied | `demand_zones[].required_volume_ML`, `demand_zones[].volume_supplied_ML` |
| Selected sources | `sources.selected[]` |
| Unused sources and reasons | `sources.unused[]` |
| Binding constraints | `binding_constraints_summary[]` and `constraints[]` |
| Treated-water quality | `water_quality.after_treatment` |
| Safety margins | `water_quality.after_treatment.*.safety_margin_percent` |
| Units | Unit fields contained in the relevant JSON objects |
| Estimated inputs and limitations | `data_flags.estimated_fields[]` |
| Sensitivity to assumptions | `sensitivity_to_key_assumptions[]` |

The evaluator must not use outside knowledge to add facts that are absent from the JSON.

## 3. Evaluation Order

Evaluation must be completed in this order:

1. Read the Results JSON.
2. Read the complete explanation.
3. Apply the critical pass/fail checks in Section 4.
4. If a critical criterion fails, record the overall outcome as **FAIL**.
5. Score the six quality criteria in Section 5 from 1 to 5.
6. Record evidence and comments for each judgment.
7. Have at least two team members independently score the reference explanation and Task 9 output.
8. Discuss meaningful differences and record the agreed final score.

A critical failure cannot be cancelled by high style scores.

## 4. Critical Pass/Fail Criteria

Each criterion must be marked `PASS`, `FAIL`, or `N/A`. Use `N/A` only when the relevant JSON section is genuinely absent and the omission is handled safely.

| ID | Critical criterion | PASS condition | FAIL condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | Correct selected sources | Every source described as selected matches `sources.selected[]`. Names, selected/unused state, volumes, and blend percentages are correct when stated. | A selected source is omitted in a supposedly complete selected-source section, an unused source is described as selected, or a stated value contradicts the JSON. |
| C2 | Correct unused sources | Every source described as unused matches `sources.unused[]`, and required unused sources are identified. | An unused source is presented as selected, a required unused source is omitted from a complete explanation, or its status is wrong. |
| C3 | Correct binding constraints | All constraints in `binding_constraints_summary[]` are reported or clearly explained without identifying non-binding constraints as binding. | A binding constraint is omitted from the binding-constraints section, a non-binding constraint is called binding, or the explanation reverses the meaning. |
| C4 | No invented numbers | Every stated number is present in the JSON or directly calculated using an explicitly stated formula. | A number is fabricated, altered, guessed, or presented without JSON support. |
| C5 | No invented reasons | Reasons for selection, exclusion, limitations, or outcomes are copied or faithfully paraphrased from JSON evidence. | The explanation invents a cause, such as contamination, reliability, or quality benefit, that is not supported by the JSON. |
| C6 | Correct quality status | PASS/FAIL/violation statements match `water_quality.after_treatment.*.status` and the reported values and limits. | A failed parameter is called safe/pass, a passed parameter is called failed, or after-blending quality is confused with after-treatment compliance. |
| C7 | Correct units | Units are copied correctly. pH is not labelled with an incorrect physical unit; alkalinity uses `mg/L CaCO3`; turbidity uses `NTU`; volume uses `ML`; cost uses `AUD`. | A unit is wrong, misleading, or attached to the wrong value. |
| C8 | Estimated fields disclosed | The explanation contains a clear limitations/estimated-fields section that discloses the material items in `data_flags.estimated_fields[]`, or accurately states that no estimated fields are listed. | Estimated inputs are presented as confirmed real measurements, the explanation contains no meaningful disclosure despite listed estimated fields, or material limitations are concealed. |
| C9 | Unsafe results are not described as safe | Any failed quality result, unmet demand, infeasible solve, or negative safety margin is clearly identified as unsafe, infeasible, or requiring review. | An unsafe, infeasible, incomplete, or failed result is described as safe, optimal for operation, or acceptable. |

### Overall critical decision

- **PASS:** Every applicable critical criterion passes.
- **FAIL:** One or more applicable critical criteria fail.

For the current reference JSON, C9 passes because all treated-water quality statuses are `PASS`, the solve status is `OPTIMAL`, and demand is fully supplied. However, this must be rechecked for every new JSON input.

## 5. Scored Criteria: 1 to 5

Scores measure quality after critical checks. Use whole numbers only.

### S1. Factual Accuracy

Measures whether statements, numerical values, optimisation decisions, statuses, and relationships accurately match the Results JSON.

| Score | Definition |
|---|---|
| **5** | All stated facts are correct, complete, and directly supported by the Results JSON. No unsupported information is included. |
| **3** | Mostly accurate, with only minor wording issues or small non-critical omissions that do not change the interpretation of the optimisation result. |
| **1** | Contains incorrect facts, invented information, unsupported claims, or contradictions with the Results JSON. |

### S2. Completeness

Measures whether the explanation covers all important optimisation outcomes required for operational understanding.

| Score | Definition |
|---|---|
| **5** | Covers all important optimisation results, including selected sources, unused sources, binding constraints, demand satisfaction, water quality, safety margins, estimated fields, and operational limitations. |
| **3** | Covers most important optimisation results but omits one or two useful details that do not significantly affect understanding. |
| **1** | Omits multiple important optimisation results or leaves out information needed for safe operational interpretation. |

### S3. Clarity

Measures whether the explanation is clear, well organised, and understandable for a non-technical operator.

| Score | Definition |
|---|---|
| **5** | Clear, concise, logically organised, and easy to understand. Technical terms are appropriately explained where necessary. |
| **3** | Generally understandable but contains some awkward wording, minor organisational issues, or unnecessary technical language. |
| **1** | Difficult to understand because of poor organisation, ambiguity, or excessive technical complexity. |

### S4. Operator Usefulness

Measures whether the explanation helps an operator understand the optimisation result and make informed decisions.

| Score | Definition |
|---|---|
| **5** | Clearly explains the selected sources, optimisation outcome, operational constraints, quality compliance, demand satisfaction, limitations, and important operational implications. |
| **3** | Provides useful operational information but lacks some supporting context or practical detail. |
| **1** | Provides little operational value or could lead to misunderstanding of the optimisation result. |

### S5. Handling of Uncertainty

Measures whether estimated data, assumptions, placeholders, and data limitations are clearly disclosed.

| Score | Definition |
|---|---|
| **5** | All estimated fields, assumptions, and important data limitations are clearly identified and appropriately explained. |
| **3** | Some estimated information is disclosed, but important limitations or assumptions are only partially explained. |
| **1** | Estimated data are presented as confirmed operational values, or uncertainty is not disclosed despite estimated fields existing in the Results JSON. |

### S6. Consistency with the Results JSON

Measures whether every important statement can be directly traced to the Results JSON and whether the explanation remains internally consistent.

| Score | Definition |
|---|---|
| **5** | Every important statement is directly supported by the Results JSON, and the explanation contains no internal inconsistencies. |
| **3** | Most statements are supported by the Results JSON, but some information is vague or only partially traceable. |
| **1** | Multiple statements cannot be verified using the Results JSON or contradict either the JSON or other parts of the explanation. |

---

## 6. Score Calculation and Final Rating

Each scored criterion contributes equally to the overall quality score.

**Total Score**

```
S1 + S2 + S3 + S4 + S5 + S6
```

**Maximum Score:** **30**

**Average Score**

```
Total Score ÷ 6
```

### Quality Bands

| Total Score | Average | Quality Level |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| **27–30** | 4.50–5.00 | Excellent |
| **23–26** | 3.83–4.49 | Good |
| **18–22** | 3.00–3.82 | Acceptable with Revisions |
| **12–17** | 2.00–2.99 | Poor |
| **6–11** | 1.00–1.99 | Unacceptable |

### Final Outcome Rule

The final outcome is determined using both the Critical Pass/Fail Criteria and the total quality score.

| Outcome | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **PASS** | All applicable critical criteria pass **and** the total score is **23–30**. |
| **REVISE** | All applicable critical criteria pass **and** the total score is **18–22**. |
| **FAIL** | One or more applicable critical criteria fail, **or** the total score is **below 18**. |

This approach ensures that factual correctness and operational safety are always prioritised over writing quality. An explanation cannot receive a PASS unless it satisfies both the critical safety checks and the required quality standard.

## 8. Example Evaluation: Convincing but Incorrect Explanation

This example demonstrates how the evaluation rubric should be applied in practice. Although the explanation below is professionally written and appears technically convincing, it does not accurately represent the optimisation results contained in the AquaBlend Results JSON.

The purpose of this example is to show that explanations should always be evaluated against the Results JSON rather than judged solely on writing quality or readability.

### Example Explanation

> *The model recommends 60% Silvan Reservoir and 40% Groundwater Bore 1. Groundwater was selected because it provides better water quality than the other available sources. All optimisation results are fully verified using operational data, and turbidity is the water-quality parameter operating closest to its regulatory limit.*

Although this explanation appears clear and technically sound, several statements contradict the optimisation results contained in the Results JSON.

### Step 1 – Apply the Critical Pass/Fail Criteria

| Critical Criterion | Result | Justification |
|--------------------|--------|---------------|
| **C1 – Correct Selected Sources** | ❌ FAIL | The Results JSON selects **Silvan Reservoir (42%)** and **Yarra River, Kew (58%)**. The explanation incorrectly replaces Yarra River with Groundwater Bore 1 and changes the blend percentages. |
| **C2 – Correct Unused Sources** | ❌ FAIL | Groundwater Bore 1 is identified as **unused** in the Results JSON but is incorrectly reported as selected. |
| **C3 – Correct Binding Constraints** | ✅ PASS | The explanation does not incorrectly describe any binding constraints. |
| **C4 – No Invented Numbers** | ❌ FAIL | The blend percentages (60% and 40%) are fabricated and do not appear anywhere in the Results JSON. |
| **C5 – No Invented Reasons** | ❌ FAIL | The explanation invents a quality-related reason for selecting Groundwater Bore 1 that is not supported by the optimisation results. |
| **C6 – Correct Water Quality Status** | ❌ FAIL | The explanation incorrectly states that turbidity is the closest parameter to its regulatory limit. The Results JSON shows that **pH** has the smallest safety margin (21.4%). |
| **C7 – Correct Units** | ✅ PASS | No incorrect measurement units are used in this example. |
| **C8 – Estimated Fields Disclosed** | ❌ FAIL | The explanation incorrectly claims that all data are operationally verified, even though several estimated fields are identified within `data_flags.estimated_fields[]`. |
| **C9 – Unsafe Results Not Reported as Safe** | ✅ PASS | No unsafe optimisation results are incorrectly described as safe. |

### Step 2 – Determine the Outcome

Several critical criteria fail during evaluation. According to the rules defined in this rubric, any applicable critical failure results in an overall **FAIL**, regardless of the writing quality or numerical quality score.

**Overall Outcome:** **FAIL**

### Why This Example Matters

This worked example demonstrates that professional writing alone is not sufficient for a high evaluation score.

An explanation must accurately represent the optimisation results contained in the Results JSON. Invented values, unsupported assumptions, omitted optimisation details, or incorrect operational conclusions may appear convincing to a reader but could mislead water system operators if presented as factual information.

The evaluation rubric therefore prioritises factual correctness, completeness, transparency, and operational usefulness over writing style. Every important statement should be directly traceable to the Results JSON before an explanation can receive a PASS outcome.

## 9. Evaluation Procedure and Independent Review Process

To ensure fair, objective, and repeatable assessment, every explanation should be evaluated using the same standard procedure.

### Step 1 – Individual Evaluation

Each reviewer should independently evaluate the explanation without discussing it with other reviewers.

The reviewer should:

1. Read the complete AquaBlend Results JSON.
2. Read the explanation being evaluated.
3. Apply all applicable Critical Pass/Fail Criteria described in Section 4.
4. If all applicable critical criteria pass, evaluate the explanation using the six Scored Criteria described in Section 5.
5. Assign scores using the defined **5–3–1 scoring scale**.
6. Calculate the total score.
7. Determine the overall outcome (**PASS**, **REVISE**, or **FAIL**).
8. Record supporting comments for each criterion where appropriate.

Independent evaluation reduces reviewer bias and encourages objective assessment based solely on evidence contained within the Results JSON.

---

### Step 2 – Comparison of Results

After all reviewers have completed their independent assessments, the evaluation results should be compared.

The reviewers should first compare:

- Critical PASS/FAIL decisions;
- Total quality scores;
- Individual criterion scores; and
- Overall evaluation outcome.

Differences should be identified before any discussion begins.

---

### Step 3 – Resolve Disagreements

Where differences exist between reviewers, each disagreement should be resolved using evidence from the Results JSON.

Discussion should focus on:

- which JSON fields support each interpretation;
- whether important optimisation information has been omitted;
- whether unsupported assumptions have been introduced; and
- whether the explanation remains useful for operational decision-making.

Reviewers should avoid changing scores based solely on personal writing preferences or stylistic differences.

---

### Step 4 – Final Agreed Evaluation

After discussion, reviewers should agree on a final evaluation.

The final recorded assessment should include:

- Critical Pass/Fail results;
- Scores for all six quality criteria;
- Total score;
- Overall outcome (PASS, REVISE, or FAIL); and
- Brief justification for any significant revisions to the original assessment.

If reviewers cannot reach agreement, the Team Lead or Product Owner should make the final decision using the Results JSON as the authoritative reference.

---

### Purpose of Independent Review

Independent review improves the reliability and consistency of the evaluation process by ensuring that explanations are assessed objectively rather than according to individual writing preferences.

Using multiple reviewers also reduces the likelihood of overlooking factual errors, missing optimisation details, or inconsistent interpretations of the Results JSON.

- accurately reports the optimisation outcome;
- correctly explains the primary reason why Groundwater Bore 1 was not selected;

- Although the explanation acknowledges that some inputs are estimated, it does not provide a complete disclosure of all estimated fields identified in `data_flags.estimated_fields[]`.

These omissions do not change the optimisation outcome, but they reduce the completeness and transparency of the explanation.

### Overall Outcome

**REVISE**

The reference explanation is factually accurate and remains fully consistent with the AquaBlend Results JSON. However, it does not completely satisfy the completeness, transparency, and operational reporting requirements defined by this evaluation rubric.

Before deployment as an operator-facing explanation, it should be revised to include the missing binding constraint, explicitly report demand satisfaction, highlight the smallest safety margin, and provide a more complete disclosure of estimated inputs and data limitations.

### Conclusion

This assessment demonstrates the purpose of the AquaBlend LLM Evaluation Rubric. The rubric evaluates explanations not only for factual correctness, but also for completeness, transparency, consistency, and operational usefulness.

An explanation may therefore be technically accurate yet still require revision if important optimisation details are omitted. By applying both the Critical Pass/Fail Criteria and the Scored Criteria, the rubric provides a structured, repeatable, and objective method for assessing explanation quality across current and future AquaBlend scenarios.
